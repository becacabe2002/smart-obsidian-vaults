# AI vs. Machine Learning vs. Deep Learning

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the overarching field of creating systems capable of mimicking human intelligence. Machine Learning (ML) is a subset of AI that enables computers to learn from data without explicit programming. Deep Learning is a further specialization of ML, utilizing multi-layered artificial neural networks to model complex data like text, images, and audio.

### Key Distinctions
- **Scope**: AI is the broad concept; ML is a subset of AI; Deep Learning is a specialized subset of ML.
- **Feature Engineering**: Machine Learning often requires human-guided feature engineering, whereas Deep Learning automatically discovers hierarchical features from raw data.
- **Requirements**: Due to its complexity, Deep Learning requires significantly more data and computational power than traditional Machine Learning.
- **Use Cases**: Deep Learning excels where patterns are highly complex, such as image recognition and natural language processing.
